Kerala - God's own country

Kerala is a state in South India on the Malabar Coast.It is a narrow strip of coastal territory that slopes down the Western Ghats in a cascade of lush green vegetation, and reaches to the Arabian sea. Spread over 38,863 sq.km, it is bordered by Karnataka to the north and northeast, Tamil Nadu to the east and south, and the Lakshadweep Sea to the west. Kerala is the thirteenth-largest Indian state by population. It is divided into 14 districts with the capital being Thiruvananthapuram. The state's coastline extends for 595 kilometres.Kochi is a major port city on the south-west coast of India. It is called the Queen of the Arabian Sea.

Malayalam is the most widely spoken language and is also the official language of the state. Hinduism is practised by more than half of the population, followed by Islam and Christianity. The culture of Kerala is composite and cosmopolitan in nature and it is an integral part of Indian culture. The mainstay of Kerala's economy is Agriculture. Tourism and Export are also a significant role in the economy of this state.

FAMOUS FOR

  • Named as one of the ten paradises of the world by National Geographic Traveller Kerala is famous especially for its ecotourism initiatives and beautiful backwaters.
  • Its unique culture and traditions, coupled with its varied demography, have made Kerala one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world.
  • It is also known for its backwaters, mountains, coconuts, coffee, cashews, spices and art forms like Kathakali and Mohini Attam.
  • In 1991, Kerala became the first state in India to be recognised as a completely literate state.
  • Kerala is known as the "Land of Spices".

TOURIST SPOTS

  1. Kerala is one of the prominent tourist destinations of India, with backwaters, hill stations, beaches, Ayurvedic tourism and tropical greenery as its major attractions.
  2. The major beaches are at Kovalam, Varkala, Cherai, Alappuzha, Payyambalam, Muzhappilangad and Bekal. Kerala is popular for having numerous majestic waterfalls.
  3. Popular hill stations are at Wayanad, Munnar, Peermade, Paithalmala, etc. Munnar is 4,500 feet above sea level and is known for tea plantations, and a variety of flora and fauna.
  4. The forts at Bekal, Palakkad and Thalassery date from the pre-British Raj era. Kerala's ecotourism destinations include 12 wildlife sanctuaries and two national parks.
  5. The hills of the Western Ghats dressed in tea and spice plantations make for a pleasing sight, while the waterfalls and wildlife present the various wonders of nature.

FOOD

  1. Kerala cuisine offers a multitude of both vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes prepared using fish, poultry and red meat with rice a typical accompaniment.
  2. Chillies, curry leaves, coconut, mustard seeds, turmeric, tamarind, and asafoetida are all frequently used.
  3. Boiled rice is a staple dish and is eaten with all sorts of gravies and curried accompaniments.

Festivals

  • Onam is a harvest festival celebrated by the people of Kerala and is reminiscent of the state's agrarian past.Many of the temples in Kerala hold festivals on specific days of the year.
  • Vishu is an auspicious festival celebrated by Hindus. It is hailed as the beginning of a new year by residents of Kerala.
  • Christmas and Easter in Kerala are celebrated unlike anywhere else in the world.

Lifestyle

  • Kerala has high standards of living compared to its neighbours and other Indian states and has social lifestyle indices such as education and healthcare that are on par with developed countries.
  • The people of Kerala, 'God's Own Country' are known to be the simplest and extremely down to earth, who lead a simple lifestyle.
  • Most of them stick to their original and traditional way of living, which is away from the glitter of the stressful city life.
  • Keralites have a lifestyle that is not at all complicated and therefore, they are content and happy owing to their unfussy pleasures of life.
